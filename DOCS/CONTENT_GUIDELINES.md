# Content Guidelines for Twelve Stones

## Purpose

This document establishes clear boundaries for age-appropriate content and biblical accuracy in *Twelve Stones*. All content must be both educationally sound and suitable for the target audience.

---

## Age Rating Target

**Primary Rating:** ESRB E (Everyone) / PEGI 3 / Apple 4+  
**Stretch Goal:** Content suitable for all ages, from children to adults

---

## Content Rating Framework

### All Ages (E - Everyone) - Primary Content

This is the default content level for the main game.

#### ✅ ALLOWED:
- **Biblical narratives** told in age-appropriate language
- **Educational content** about history, culture, and scripture
- **Positive themes:** Faith, hope, love, redemption, forgiveness, courage
- **Mild challenges** in mini-games (difficulty, not content-based)
- **Implied conflict** without graphic depiction
- **Historical context** presented sensitively
- **Character flaws** shown as learning opportunities
- **Cultural accuracy** (clothing, architecture, customs)
- **Natural consequences** of choices in biblical stories
- **Celebration** of victories and achievements

#### ❌ PROHIBITED:
- **Graphic violence** of any kind
- **Blood or gore**
- **Detailed descriptions** of battles or deaths
- **Frightening imagery** that could scare children
- **Romantic or sexual content**
- **Crude humor or language**
- **Gambling mechanics** (loot boxes, pay-to-win)
- **Alcohol or substance use** (even historically accurate)
- **Occult practices** (even in negative context)
- **Nightmare-inducing** imagery or scenarios
- **Graphic descriptions** of punishment or suffering

## Handling Difficult Biblical Topics

### Violence in Scripture

Many biblical narratives include violence (battles, judgment, etc.). Here's how to handle it:

#### General Principles:
```
❌ DON'T: "Simeon and Levi slaughtered the men of Shechem"
✅ DO: "Simeon and Levi took revenge in anger, which Jacob condemned"

❌ DON'T: Show blood, weapons, or combat animations
✅ DO: Use symbolic representation (crossed swords icon fading)

❌ DON'T: Glorify or detail violence
✅ DO: Focus on consequences and lessons learned
```

#### Specific Examples:

**Simeon and Levi at Shechem (Genesis 34):**
- **Mention:** Their actions were hasty and condemned by Jacob
- **Focus on:** The lesson about controlled anger and justice vs. revenge
- **Omit:** Graphic details of the event
- **Show:** Jacob's disappointment and the consequences

**Judah's Military Leadership:**
- **Mention:** Judah led in battles to protect Israel
- **Focus on:** Courage, strategy, wisdom, protection of the innocent
- **Omit:** Battle details, casualties
- **Show:** Leadership qualities and responsibility

**Tribal Conflicts:**
- **Mention:** Disagreements and challenges existed
- **Focus on:** Resolution, unity, working together
- **Omit:** Specific violent incidents
- **Show:** Reconciliation and growth

### Death and Mortality

Biblical stories include death, but it must be handled carefully:

```
✅ APPROPRIATE:
- "Jacob gathered his sons before he passed away"
- "Moses saw the promised land before his journey ended"
- "David mourned when his child died"
- "The elders teach us before they rest with their ancestors"

❌ INAPPROPRIATE:
- Graphic descriptions of death
- Disturbing imagery
- Detailed descriptions of illness
- Focus on physical suffering
```

### Sin and Consequences

Biblical accuracy requires acknowledging sin without glorifying it:

#### Reuben's Sin (Genesis 35:22):
```
✅ APPROPRIATE:
"Reuben made a serious mistake that cost him his birthright. 
Even the firstborn can lose their blessing through poor choices."

❌ INAPPROPRIATE:
Explicit mention of the specific sin (inappropriate for children)
```

#### Other Examples:
- **Focus on:** The lesson and God's redemption
- **Show:** Consequences leading to repentance
- **Emphasize:** God's grace and forgiveness
- **Avoid:** Graphic or explicit descriptions

### Judgment and Divine Wrath

When God's judgment appears in tribal history:

```
✅ APPROPRIATE:
- "God's people faced consequences when they turned away from Him"
- "The tribe learned that obedience brings blessing"
- "Through trials, they grew stronger in faith"

❌ INAPPROPRIATE:
- Detailed descriptions of plagues or disasters
- Vengeful or harsh portrayal of God
- Fear-based theology
- Emphasis on punishment over redemption
```

## Biblical Accuracy Checklist

Before approving any content, verify:

### Historical Accuracy
- [ ] Events align with biblical timeline
- [ ] Geography is correct (tribal territories, landmarks)
- [ ] Cultural practices are historically accurate
- [ ] Architecture and clothing appropriate to period
- [ ] Names are spelled and pronounced correctly
- [ ] No anachronisms (modern concepts in ancient setting)

### Theological Soundness
- [ ] Scripture is quoted accurately from approved translation
- [ ] Interpretations are orthodox and mainstream
- [ ] Respects both Jewish and Christian perspectives
- [ ] Avoids denominational controversies
- [ ] Shows God's character as loving, just, and merciful
- [ ] Emphasizes grace alongside law

### Character Representation
- [ ] Biblical figures portrayed with appropriate reverence
- [ ] Strengths and weaknesses balanced
- [ ] Actions align with biblical accounts
- [ ] Dialogue feels authentic to time period
- [ ] Character growth shown over time
- [ ] Positive role models emphasized

### Scripture Usage
- [ ] Verses quoted in full (not partial to change meaning)
- [ ] Context provided for each verse
- [ ] Translation specified and permission obtained if needed
- [ ] Proper attribution (book, chapter, verse)
- [ ] No verses taken out of context
- [ ] Memorization aids are accurate

## Language Guidelines

### Approved Language Tone:
- **Reverent** but not archaic (avoid "thee/thou" unless in scripture quote)
- **Clear** and accessible to young readers
- **Engaging** without being casual about sacred things
- **Educational** without being preachy
- **Inclusive** of different traditions

### Examples:

```
✅ GOOD:
"God made a covenant with Abraham and his descendants."
"The Levites served in the temple with dedication."
"Joseph trusted God even when life was difficult."

❌ AVOID:
"God is, like, totally awesome and stuff."
"The priests did their thing at the temple."
"Joseph was super bummed but whatever."
```

## Visual Content Standards

### Character Design:
- **Modest clothing** appropriate to period
- **Diverse appearances** reflecting historical accuracy
- **Dignified portrayal** of biblical figures
- **Age-appropriate** character models
- **Cultural respect** in design choices

### Environments:
- **Historically accurate** architecture
- **Beautiful** but not idealized beyond recognition
- **Safe feeling** spaces, even in challenges
- **Educational** details (artifacts, tools, customs)
- **No dark or frightening** areas

### UI/Visual Effects:
- **Clean** and readable
- **Positive feedback** animations (celebrations, encouragement)
- **No frightening** transition effects
- **Colorblind accessible**
- **Biblical aesthetic** maintained

---

## Audio Content Standards

### Music:
- **Original compositions** or licensed appropriately
- **Middle Eastern instruments** for authenticity
- **Peaceful** and encouraging tone
- **Appropriate** for worship context in certain scenes
- **No jarring** or scary sound effects

### Voice Acting (if implemented):
- **Professional** quality
- **Age-appropriate** voice actors for characters
- **Clear** pronunciation of Hebrew words
- **Respectful** tone
- **Multiple language** options considered

### Sound Effects:
- **Positive** audio feedback
- **No scary** sounds
- **Celebratory** for achievements
- **Gentle** for errors or failures
- **Authentic** but not overwhelming

---

## Multiplayer Content Standards

### Social Features:
- **Chat:** Disabled or heavily moderated
- **Usernames:** Filtered for inappropriate content
- **Reporting:** Easy reporting of inappropriate behavior
- **Privacy:** COPPA compliant, no personal data shared
- **Safe environment:** Adult moderation

### Competitive Elements:
- **Encourage** cooperation over competition
- **Celebrate** all players, not just winners
- **No toxic** behavior tolerated
- **Teach** good sportsmanship
- **Include** everyone regardless of skill level

---

## Educational Content Standards

### Learning Objectives:
Each tribal quest should teach:
- **Historical fact** about the tribe
- **Biblical principle** or virtue
- **Scripture verse** (memorization)
- **Cultural context** of ancient Israel
- **Spiritual lesson** applicable today

### Educational Approach:
- **Integrated** into gameplay, not forced
- **Interactive** rather than lecture-style
- **Reinforced** through repetition and review
- **Tested** through gentle quizzes
- **Rewarded** with achievement recognition

---

## Special Considerations

### Hebrew Language and Culture:
- **Hebrew words** transliterated and pronounced clearly
- **Cultural practices** explained respectfully
- **Jewish heritage** honored
- **No stereotypes** or caricatures
- **Consultation** with cultural experts

### Christian Interpretations:
- **Messianic connections** mentioned appropriately
- **New Testament** references kept separate (future expansion)
- **Respectful** of Jewish perspective
- **No supersessionism** (replacement theology)
- **Inclusive** of all Christian traditions

### Interfaith Respect:
- **Jewish players** should feel respected
- **Christian players** find spiritual meaning
- **General audience** learns without pressure
- **No proselytizing** in inappropriate ways
- **Educational** first, inspirational second

---

## Content Review Process

### Before Implementation:
1. **Writer** creates content following guidelines
2. **Biblical scholar** reviews for accuracy
3. **Child development expert** reviews for age-appropriateness
4. **Cultural consultant** reviews for sensitivity
5. **Legal review** for copyright/trademark issues
6. **Playtesting** with target demographic
7. **Final approval** by content committee

### Red Flags (Require Additional Review):
- Any depiction of violence
- Difficult theological concepts
- Cultural elements from outside team's expertise
- New scripture citations
- Character dialogue with God
- Miraculous events
- Controversial interpretations
