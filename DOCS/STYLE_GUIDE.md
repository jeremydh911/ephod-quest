# Style Guide for Twelve Stones

## Overview

This document establishes the visual, audio, and narrative style for *Twelve Stones*, ensuring a consistent and immersive biblical aesthetic throughout the game.

---

## Core Aesthetic: "Earth & Gold"

The game's visual identity is built around warm, earthy tones complemented by rich golden accents, evoking the ancient Middle Eastern setting and the sacred nature of the journey.

### Primary Color Palette

```
Earth Tones (Base):
  - Desert Sand:     #E9D5B3  (Backgrounds, UI base)
  - Clay Brown:      #8B6F47  (Ground, structures)
  - Olive Green:     #6B7A4F  (Vegetation, accents)
  - Terracotta:      #C5704B  (Pottery, roofs)
  - Stone Gray:      #9B9B8C  (Rocks, walls)

Gold Tones (Accent):
  - Pure Gold:       #FFD700  (Highlights, divine elements)
  - Antique Gold:    #C5A572  (UI borders, achievements)
  - Bronze:          #CD7F32  (Completion rank)
  - Amber:           #FFBF00  (Light sources, warmth)

Sacred Elements:
  - Deep Purple:     #4B3F72  (Priestly garments, Levi)
  - Royal Blue:      #2E4272  (Judah, leadership)
  - Crimson:         #8B0000  (Tabernacle curtains)
  - White Linen:     #F5F5DC  (Purity, scripture backgrounds)

Tribal Accent Colors (Secondary):
  - Reuben:          #8B4513  (Saddle Brown)
  - Simeon:          #696969  (Dim Gray)
  - Levi:            #4B0082  (Indigo)
  - Judah:           #DAA520  (Goldenrod)
  - Dan:             #2F4F4F  (Dark Slate Gray)
  - Naphtali:        #87CEEB  (Sky Blue)
  - Gad:             #556B2F  (Dark Olive Green)
  - Asher:           #FFD700  (Gold)
  - Issachar:        #191970  (Midnight Blue)

  - Joseph:          #228B22  (Forest Green)

```

### Usage Guidelines

```
Backgrounds:
  - Primary: Desert Sand gradient to Clay Brown
  - Sky: Warm peachy tones transitioning to light blue
  - Interiors: Darker earth tones with golden lighting

UI Elements:
  - Buttons: Clay Brown with Antique Gold borders
  - Active/Hover: Pure Gold highlights
  - Disabled: Desaturated earth tones
  - Text: Dark Stone Gray on light backgrounds, White Linen on dark

Sacred/Important Elements:
  - Stones: Each tribal stone in its accent color with gold shimmer
  - Scripture Display: White Linen background with Gold border
  - Elder Characters: Robes in earth tones with tribal color accents
  - Divine Light: Amber/Pure Gold glows and halos
```

## Visual Style

### Art Direction

**Overall Aesthetic:** Painterly 2D art with slight texture, reminiscent of ancient manuscripts and illuminated texts.

**Influences:**
- Ancient Middle Eastern art
- Illuminated manuscripts
- Biblical illustrations (traditional, non-idolatrous)
- Minimalist modern design for clarity

### Character Design

#### Player Character
```
Style:
  - Simple, iconic silhouette
  - Modest biblical-era clothing (tunic, sandals, headwrap)
  - Gender-neutral or selectable options
  - Minimal facial features (avoiding idol-like appearance)
  - Age: Young adult representation

Colors:
  - Neutral earth tones (customizable with tribal colors)
  - Cloth texture visible but simplified
  
Animation:
  - Walk cycle: 4-6 frames
  - Idle: Gentle breathing animation
  - Celebrate: Arms raised, jumping
  - Thoughtful: Hand on chin pose
```

#### Elder Characters
```
Style:
  - Distinguished, respected appearance
  - Long robes with tribal color accents
  - Beards (cultural accuracy)
  - Aged features showing wisdom
  - Distinct silhouette per tribe

Tribal Elder Distinctions:
  - Reuben: Weathered, resigned expression
  - Simeon: Strong, justice-focused stance
  - Levi: Priestly garments, holding instruments
  - Judah: Regal bearing, lion emblem
  - Dan: Thoughtful, scales or gavel nearby
  - Naphtali: Lithe, energetic posture
  - Gad: Warrior stance, protective
  - Asher: Joyful, surrounded by crops
  - Issachar: Scholarly, star charts visible
  - Zebulun: Sailor's attire, nautical elements
  - Joseph: Fruitful imagery, prosperous appearance
  - Benjamin: Youngest energy, protective gear

Animation:
  - Gentle gestures while speaking
  - Nodding, pointing
  - Blessing gesture (hands raised)
```

### Environment Design

#### World Map
```
Style:
  - Ancient map aesthetic (parchment texture)
  - Illustrated territories with tribal symbols
  - Mountain ranges, rivers (Jordan), Dead Sea
  - Paths connecting tribal areas
  - Decorative borders with gold filigree

Interactive Elements:
  - Glowing tribal territories (unlocked/locked)
  - Animated paths revealing as progress
  - Stone markers showing completion
  - Gentle parallax scrolling
```

#### Tribal Territories
```
Each territory has distinct visual identity:

Reuben (East of Jordan):
  - Rocky, mountainous terrain
  - Cooler color temperature
  - Ladder iconography subtle in background

Simeon (Southern region):
  - Desert environment
  - Scales/balance imagery
  - Fortified settlements

Levi (No specific territory - temple):
  - Temple courtyard aesthetic
  - Musical instruments visible
  - Priestly elements throughout

Judah (Central region):
  - Regal architecture
  - Lion motifs in stonework
  - Golden accents prominent

Dan (Northern region):
  - Lush vegetation
  - Hidden elements to discover
  - Serpent imagery (tasteful, biblical)

Naphtali (Northern mountains):
  - Rolling hills
  - Deer/doe imagery
  - Open, free feeling spaces

Gad (East of Jordan):
  - Fortified positions
  - Defensive structures
  - Warrior training grounds

Asher (Northern coast):
  - Fertile fields
  - Olive groves
  - Abundant harvest imagery

Issachar (Central plains):
  - Observatory platforms
  - Night sky prominent
  - Scholarly atmosphere

Zebulun (Coast):
  - Harbor scenes
  - Ships and sails
  - Water prominent

Joseph (Central region):
  - Garden of Eden-like abundance
  - Growing plants and trees
  - Colorful, lush environment

Benjamin (Central region):
  - Protective walls
  - Youngest brother imagery
  - Treasured, secure feeling
```

## UI/UX Design

### Scripture Scroll Aesthetic

All UI elements should feel like ancient scrolls and parchments:

```
Menu Backgrounds:
  - Rolled parchment texture
  - Torn or aged edges
  - Subtle vignette around borders
  - Gold wax seal decorations

Buttons:
  - Raised clay tablet appearance
  - Cuneiform-inspired decorative borders
  - Press animation: Slight shadow/depth change
  - Hover: Golden glow around edges

Text Boxes:
  - Inset papyrus texture
  - Handwritten-style font (but highly readable)
  - Gold illuminated letters for headers
  - Decorative capitals for paragraphs

Progress Indicators:
  - Filling oil lamp (ancient light source)
  - Growing vine (fruitfulness theme)
  - Stacking stones (memorial theme)
  - Scrolling scripture scroll
```

### HUD (Heads-Up Display)

```
During Gameplay:
  - Minimal on-screen elements
  - Semi-transparent earth-toned panels
  - Corner placement (not center-obscuring)
  
Elements:
  - Score: Stylized number with small stone icons
  - Timer: Hourglass or sundial visualization
  - Lives/Attempts: Stone hearts or memorial stones
  - Combo Meter: Growing vine or filling chalice
  
Transitions:
  - Page turn animations for scene changes
  - Unfurling scroll for text reveals
  - Dust/sand particles for desert scenes
  - Golden light for divine moments
```

### Typography

#### Primary UI Font
```
Font Family: Noto Sans (or similar clean sans-serif)
Use Cases: Buttons, labels, scores, menus

Sizes:
  - Headers: 48-64px (Bold)
  - Subheaders: 32-40px (Semi-Bold)
  - Body: 20-24px (Regular)
  - Small Text: 16-18px (Regular)

Colors:
  - Primary Text: #3E2723 (Very Dark Brown)
  - Secondary Text: #6B5B4D (Medium Brown)
  - Disabled: #9B9B8C (Light Gray-Brown)
```

#### Scripture Font
```
Font Family: Noto Serif or Crimson Text
Use Cases: Bible verses, spiritual content, elder dialogue

Sizes:
  - Verse Display: 28-36px (Regular)
  - In-game Quotes: 20-24px (Italic)
  - Reference Citations: 16-18px (Regular)

Colors:
  - Scripture Text: #1A1A1A (Near Black)
  - Emphasis Words: #8B4513 (Saddle Brown)
  - Reference: #696969 (Dim Gray)
```

#### Hebrew Font
```
Font Family: Noto Sans Hebrew or Frank Ruhl Libre
Use Cases: Hebrew words, names, transliterations

Sizes: Match corresponding Latin text
Direction: RTL support enabled
Colors: Same as primary UI font
```

## Animation Guidelines

### Character Animation

```
Frame Rate: 12-15 FPS (stylized, not realistic)
Style: Slight easing, smooth but not floaty

Player:
  - Walk: 6 frames, medium pace
  - Run: 4 frames, faster pace
  - Jump: 3-4 frames with arc
  - Idle: 8-10 frames, gentle breathing
  - Celebrate: 6 frames, jumping/raising arms

Elders:
  - Idle: Gentle swaying, 12 frames
  - Speaking: Subtle head nods, mouth shapes
  - Blessing: 8 frames, arms raising
  - Transition: Fade in/out with golden particles
```

### UI Animation

```
Timing: Snappy but not instant (0.2-0.3 seconds)

Buttons:
  - Hover: Scale 1.0 → 1.05 (0.2s)
  - Press: Scale 1.05 → 0.95 → 1.0 (0.3s)
  - Glow: Pulse alpha 0.5 → 1.0 (0.5s loop)

Transitions:
  - Scene Change: Fade to white/gold (0.5s)
  - Menu Open: Slide in from bottom (0.3s ease-out)
  - Menu Close: Slide out to bottom (0.3s ease-in)
  - Popup: Scale from 0.8 → 1.0 with fade (0.3s)

Feedback:
  - Success: Burst of gold particles
  - Failure: Gentle shake (3 frames)
  - Collection: Item flies to UI position (0.5s curve)
  - Achievement: Banner slides in from top (0.4s)
```

### Particle Effects

```
Golden Light (Divine/Sacred):
  - Small sparkles floating upward
  - Warm glow emanating from stones
  - Radial light beams on achievements

Earth Elements:
  - Dust clouds on character landing
  - Sand particles in desert scenes
  - Leaves falling in garden areas
  - Water ripples near streams

Celebration:
  - Confetti-like flower petals
  - Golden sparks bursting
  - Gentle light rays from above
```

## Audio Style

### Music Composition

#### Instrumentation (Middle Eastern Focus)
```
Primary Instruments:
  - Oud (Middle Eastern lute)
  - Ney (reed flute)
  - Qanun (zither)
  - Darbuka (goblet drum)
  - Frame drums
  - Harp (Biblical kinnor)
  - Shofar (for dramatic moments)

Supporting:
  - String ensemble (minimal)
  - Choir (wordless vocals)
  - Acoustic guitar (subtle)

Avoid:
  - Modern synthesizers (unless very subtle)
  - Rock/pop instruments
  - Overly orchestral (keep intimate)
```

#### Musical Themes

```
Main Theme:
  - Hopeful, inspirational
  - Medium tempo (90-100 BPM)
  - Memorable melody
  - 3-4 minute loop

World Map:
  - Ambient, exploratory
  - Gentle rhythms
  - Invites contemplation
  - 4-5 minute loop

Tribal Themes (Unique per tribe):
  - Reuben: Melancholic but redemptive
  - Simeon: Strong, rhythmic, justice theme
  - Levi: Worshipful, priestly
  - Judah: Regal, bold, lion-like strength
  - Dan: Mysterious, thoughtful
  - Naphtali: Light, swift, free-flowing
  - Gad: Martial, protective
  - Asher: Joyful, abundant
  - Issachar: Contemplative, wise
  - Zebulun: Flowing like water
  - Joseph: Triumphant growth theme
  - Benjamin: Youthful energy, protective

Victory/Completion:
  - Celebratory but reverent
  - Building intensity
  - Shofar blast climax
  - 1-2 minute piece

Menu Music:
  - Calm, welcoming
  - Loops seamlessly
  - Not distracting
```

### Sound Design

#### UI Sounds
```
Button Click: Gentle tap on clay tablet (short, clean)
Button Hover: Subtle high chime (barely audible)
Menu Open: Scroll unfurling (papery sound)
Menu Close: Scroll rolling up
Success Chime: Bright harp pluck with shimmer
Error Sound: Soft, gentle "deny" tone (not harsh)
Achievement: Triumphant flourish (2-3 seconds)
```

#### Gameplay Sounds
```
Stone Collection: 
  - Stone clinking
  - Golden shimmer/magic sound
  - Satisfying "thunk"

Scripture Display:
  - Gentle chime
  - Pages turning
  - Whisper of wind

Mini-game Specific:
  - Reuben: Wood creaking (ladder)
  - Levi: Instrument sounds (harp, tambourine)
  - Judah: Lion roar (dignified, not scary)
  - Dan: Reveal chime (discovery)
  - Naphtali: Footsteps, jumping whoosh
  - Gad: Shield impacts (not violent)
  - Asher: Crop harvesting sounds
  - Issachar: Ethereal cosmic tones
  - Zebulun: Water, sails, gentle waves
  - Joseph: Growing vines, nature
  - Benjamin: Swoosh of projectiles
```

#### Ambient Sounds
```
Desert: Light wind, distant bird calls
Marketplace: Gentle crowd murmur, distant voices
Temple: Reverent quiet, footsteps echoing
Coast: Gentle waves, seagulls
Garden: Birds, rustling leaves, water

Volume: Keep very subtle, 20-30% of music volume
```

## World-Building Guidelines

### Historical Setting
```
Time Period: Bronze Age to early Iron Age (roughly 1400-1000 BCE)
Location: Ancient Israel/Canaan
Culture: Tribal confederation transitioning to kingdom

Accuracy Priorities:
1. Biblical text (primary source)
2. Archaeological evidence
3. Historical scholarship
4. Cultural sensitivity
5. Educational value
```

### Environmental Details

#### Architecture
```
Style: Ancient Middle Eastern
Materials: Mud brick, stone, wood (scarce)
Structures:
  - Simple rectangular dwellings
  - Tents for pastoral scenes
  - Fortified city walls (later tribes)
  - Temple/tabernacle elements (Levi)

Details:
  - Flat roofs with external stairs
  - Small windows (climate control)
  - Courtyards (communal living)
  - Storage jars, water vessels
```

#### Daily Life Props
```
Tools & Items:
  - Clay pottery (jars, bowls)
  - Woven baskets
  - Oil lamps
  - Grinding stones
  - Looms (weaving)
  - Agricultural tools (simple)
  - Walking staffs
  - Water skins

Decorative:
  - Woven rugs/textiles
  - Carved wood (minimal)
  - Stone carvings (no idols)
  - Dyed fabrics (expensive)
```

#### Flora & Fauna
```
Plants:
  - Olive trees
  - Date palms
  - Fig trees
  - Grape vines
  - Wheat/barley fields
  - Desert scrub vegetation
  - Acacia trees

Animals (Background only):
  - Sheep/goats (pastoral)
  - Donkeys
  - Camels
  - Doves
  - Deer/gazelles
  - Fish (coastal scenes)
  
Note: No frightening predators, family-friendly
```

## Character Creation Guidelines

### Diversity & Representation

```
Physical Appearance:
  - Middle Eastern features primary
  - Skin tones: Range of olive to dark brown (regional variation)
  - Hair: Dark brown to black, curly to wavy
  - Body types: Various but historically appropriate
  - Ages: Youth to elderly represented

Avoid:
  - Western European features as default
  - Stereotypical or caricatured features
  - Overly idealized/unrealistic proportions
  - Anachronistic fashion choices
```

### Clothing

```
Men:
  - Tunic (knee to ankle length)
  - Cloak/robe (outer garment)
  - Belt (functional and decorative)
  - Headcovering (turban or cloth)
  - Sandals (simple leather)

Women (if included):
  - Long tunic/dress
  - Outer cloak
  - Head covering (modest)
  - Sandals
  - Simple jewelry (historically appropriate)

Priestly (Levi):
  - White linen tunic
  - Colored sash
  - Head covering (turban)
  - Special garments (simplified, respectful)

Elders:
  - More elaborate robes
  - Tribal color accents
  - Symbols of authority (staff, etc.)
  - Quality fabrics indicated by texture
```

## Icon & Symbol Design

### Tribal Symbols

Each tribe has a unique symbol based on Jacob's blessings:

```
Reuben: Mandrake plant or water ripples (instability)
Simeon: Crossed swords (justice, later redemption)
Levi: Harp or incense (worship, priesthood)
Judah: Lion (strength, kingship)
Dan: Scales or serpent (judgment, discernment)
Naphtali: Deer/doe (swiftness, freedom)
Gad: Shield (defense, fortitude)
Asher: Olive branch with fruit (blessing, abundance)
Issachar: Star constellation (wisdom, timing)
Zebulun: Ship or anchor (haven, seafaring)
Joseph: Fruitful tree/vine (fruitfulness)
Benjamin: Wolf or bow (protection, skill)

Style:
  - Simple, iconic design
  - Recognizable at small sizes
  - Gold on tribal accent color background
  - Circular or shield-shaped frame
```

### UI Icons

```
Navigation:
  - Map: Unfurled scroll with landmarks
  - Settings: Gear/cog (anachronistic but functional)
  - Help: Question mark in scroll
  - Back: Left-pointing arrow
  
Gameplay:
  - Stone/Memorial: Stacked rocks
  - Scripture: Open scroll/book
  - Achievement: Wax seal with ribbon
  - Progress: Filling oil lamp
  - Multiplayer: Two figures side by side
  - Pause: Ancient pause symbol (two pillars)
  
Style:
  - Line weight: 3-4px
  - Rounded corners
  - Consistent visual weight
  - Functional first, decorative second
```
